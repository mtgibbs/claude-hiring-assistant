# Engineering Manager Competency Matrix

## Level Mapping for Hiring

| Our Hiring Rubric | Internal Level | Typical Experience |
|-------------------|----------------|--------------------|
| Engineering Manager | **G7 – Technology Manager** | 3-5 years management |
| Senior Engineering Manager | **G8 – Senior Technology Manager** | 5-8 years management |

**Note**: When evaluating manager candidates, map their experience and capabilities to our internal levels:
- **Engineering Manager candidates** should demonstrate G7 competencies
- **Senior Engineering Manager candidates** should demonstrate G8 competencies

---

## Overview

Each level is assessed across six dimensions:

1. **Scope & Management Impact**
   *Central question: What is the breadth of my responsibility and influence?*

2. **Communication**
   *Central question: How effectively do I communicate across all levels of the organization?*

3. **Systems Thinking**
   *Central question: Do I understand and anticipate the broader impact of decisions?*

4. **Execution and Delivery**
   *Central question: How effectively do I drive results and remove blockers?*

5. **Growth Mindset**
   *Central question: How do I develop myself and embrace change?*

6. **Building & Optimizing Teams**
   *Central question: How do I build, develop, and optimize high-performing teams?*

---

## G7 – Technology Manager ⭐ (Engineering Manager Hire)



**Typical Role**: First-line manager

**Hiring target**: Candidates applying for **Engineering Manager** positions should demonstrate these competencies.

### Scope & Management Impact
- Sets and manages expectations at a broader scale and complexity
- Course corrects as needed
- Provides regular feedback and manages performance concerns
- Participates in headcount planning; responsible for hiring and developing team members

### Communication
- Skilled at communicating strategy and vision; inspires team members
- Interacts and collaborates with stakeholders across departments and externally
- Listens attentively, understands concerns, and provides constructive feedback
- Transparent and open in communication, including project and organizational updates

### Systems Thinking
- Identifies all dependencies in the system when considering work or direction
- Anticipates impact of team's work and articulates it to senior management and engineers
- Coaches others to distill complex problems into simpler components
- Understands and empathizes with team members' perspectives and challenges

### Execution and Delivery
- Leverages diverse data points to inform decisions
- Removes blockers once dependencies are identified
- Demonstrates consistent delivery of large projects; iteratively builds stakeholder trust
- Understands direction and speed needed for effective outcomes

### Growth Mindset
- Self-aware and understands areas of development
- Adapts quickly to changes; implements thoughtful change initiatives
- Actively seeks feedback from team, peers, and superiors
- Delivers hard feedback when needed

### Building & Optimizing Teams
- Identifies when to delegate and when to step in
- Identifies tensions in operating structures and proposes solutions
- Forecasts and identifies headcount needs
- Mentors team members in setting ambitious career goals and connects them with opportunities

## G8 – Senior Technology Manager ⭐⭐ (Senior Engineering Manager Hire)

**Typical Role**: Second-line manager; responsible for a product or multiple sub-teams

**Hiring target**: Candidates applying for **Senior Engineering Manager** positions should demonstrate these competencies.

### Scope & Management Impact
- Participates in strategic planning with senior leaders
- Has control over planning, hiring, and implementing organizational changes
- Aligns day-to-day execution with top objectives
- Leads through other managers, orchestrating success across teams

### Communication
- Communicates strategically, aligning with organizational goals
- Breaks down complex concepts for diverse audiences
- Actively listens and seeks to understand others' perspectives
- Communicates effectively with executives, clients, and cross-functional teams

### Systems Thinking
- Understands downstream effects of actions across groups
- Anticipates unintended consequences and course corrects
- Builds processes and workflows that simplify team operations
- Extrapolates core tenets of complex challenges and explains them clearly

### Execution and Delivery
- Uses pragmatic thinking to align goals with achievable targets
- Efficiently drives outcomes aligned with strategic goals
- Critically prioritizes high-impact work and drives outcomes with speed
- Resolves conflicts constructively and teaches others to coach through complex situations

### Growth Mindset
- Cultivates a culture of open feedback and uses it to refine leadership
- Demonstrates curiosity to build trust and improve decision quality
- Sees change as opportunity; proactively manages change with contingency plans
- Encourages experimentation and treats failures as learning opportunities

### Building & Optimizing Teams
- Inspires and motivates teams
- Identifies high performers and future leaders; proactively develops them
- Solid behavioral interviewer; identifies high-quality talent
- Delegates to provide growth opportunities and coaches for effective outcomes
- Proactively checks in on team morale and cohesion

## Using This Matrix During Evaluation

### Resume Screening
When scoring resumes, look for evidence of competencies at the target level:

**For Engineering Manager (G7) candidates:**
- Scope: First-line management experience, hiring and performance management
- Communication: Evidence of stakeholder collaboration and transparent communication
- Systems: Identifies dependencies and coaches others through complexity
- Execution: Consistent delivery track record, removes blockers
- Growth: Seeks feedback, delivers difficult feedback when needed
- Teams: Mentoring evidence, delegation skills, headcount planning

**For Senior Engineering Manager (G8) candidates:**
- Scope: Multi-team leadership, strategic planning participation, organizational changes
- Communication: Executive-level communication, complex concept translation
- Systems: Anticipates downstream effects, builds simplifying processes
- Execution: Strategic alignment, high-impact prioritization, conflict resolution
- Growth: Cultivates feedback culture, manages change proactively
- Teams: Develops future leaders, strong interviewing skills, morale management

### Interview Assessment
During interviews, probe for demonstrations of these competencies:
- Ask about managing performance issues and delivering hard feedback (Scope & Growth)
- Explore cross-functional collaboration examples (Communication)
- Discuss complex project planning and dependency management (Systems Thinking)
- Inquire about removing blockers and driving delivery (Execution)
- Understand how they develop team members and identify talent (Building Teams)
- Assess their approach to change management and learning from failures (Growth Mindset)

### Red Flags
- **Insufficient management experience**: Claims manager title but only supervised 1-2 people or acted as tech lead
- **No people development**: No evidence of hiring, performance management, or mentoring
- **Lack of strategic thinking**: Only discusses tactical execution, no systems thinking
- **Poor communication examples**: Can't articulate clear vision or stakeholder management
- **Avoids conflict**: No examples of delivering difficult feedback or managing performance issues
- **Single dimension focus**: Strong technically but weak on people/communication aspects

### Calibration Notes
- Experience ranges are guidelines based on typical career progression
- Focus on demonstrated competencies across all six dimensions, not just technical strength
- Cultural differences may affect how candidates describe their work (refer to MANAGER_EVALUATION_RUBRIC.md)
- A strong G7 candidate with growth potential is better than a weak G8 candidate
- Manager candidates should show balance across all dimensions - red flag if only strong in 2-3 areas
